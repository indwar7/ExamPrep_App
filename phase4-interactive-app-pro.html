What is Science?

Science is a way of thinking, observing, and doing things to understand the world we live in. It's fundamentally about curiosity—asking questions about everything around us and systematically finding answers through investigation.

🔑 Key Concept:

Science is not just memorizing facts. It's a process of exploration, questioning, and discovery that anyone can practice.

The Scientific Method: 5-Step Process

All scientists follow a systematic approach to solve problems and discover new knowledge. This five-step method is the foundation of all scientific thinking:

1
Observe Look carefully at something interesting or puzzling that captures your curiosity.
2
Wonder Ask yourself "Why?" or "How?" about what you've observed.
3
Guess Make an educated guess (hypothesis) about the answer to your question.
4
Test Do an experiment or make more observations to check your guess.
5
Analyze Look at your results. Was your guess correct? What did you learn?

Science is Everywhere

Science isn't limited to laboratories and research centers. Real people in everyday professions use the scientific method to solve problems and make discoveries:

👨‍🍳 Chef

Wondering why certain cooking techniques work better than others

🔧 Mechanic

Troubleshooting why a vehicle isn't working properly

⚡ Electrician

Figuring out electrical problems systematically

🌾 Farmer

Testing different methods to improve crop yields

💡 Remember:

Anyone who follows the scientific method is working like a scientist! When you ask questions and find answers systematically, you're practicing science in your daily life.

Scientific Method = Observe → Wonder → Guess → Test → Analyze
Curiosity = The most important quality for any scientist
Hypothesis = An educated guess you can test through experimentation

**Step 1: Observation**

- Icon: Magnifying glass or magnifier symbol
- Text: "Observe something interesting"

**Step 2: Question**

**Step 3: Hypothesis/Guess**
- Image: Light bulb appearing
- Icon: Light bulb
- Text: "Guess a possible answer"

**Step 4: Testing**
- Image: Hands conducting experiment, performing test
- Icon: Test tubes or experiment apparatus
- Text: "Test through experiments"

**Step 5: Analysis**
- Image: Person writing conclusions, taking notes
- Icon: Notepad or graph

**Arrows**: Connecting steps in sequence
**Colors**: Each step in different color for clarity

### Illustration 3.2: "Case Study The Pen Problem"
**Description**: Step by step visual showing the broken pen example
**Sequence**:
1. Pen that won't write
2. Child noticing and wondering (question mark)
3. Child guessing "Ink is finished" (thought bubble with ink bottle)
4. Opening the pen and checking (action shot)
5. Empty ink refill shown (result)
6. Conclusion: "The ink finished" (checkmark)
7. Alternative: "If ink was not finished, make another guess"
8. New guess: "Ink might be dried up"
9. Testing again
**Caption**: "How we solve a real problem using the scientific method"

**Colors**: Engaging and clear
**Size**: Full page or spread

## ILLUSTRATION SPECIFICATIONS

### Style Guidelines
- Age appropriate for Grade 6 students (11 12 years old)
- Mix of realistic and stylized/cartoon elements
- Clear and easily identifiable objects
- Inclusive representation of different people, cultures, and genders
- Engaging and encouraging curiosity
- Good color contrast for visibility
- Not overly complex or cluttered

### Color Palette
- Vibrant and engaging colors
- Appropriate use of color psychology
  - Blues and greens for nature
  - Reds and yellows for energy and warmth
  - Purples and pinks for creativity and curiosity

### Technical Specifications
- Resolution: 300 DPI for print
- Format: Suitable for textbook layout
- Size: A4 or spreads as required
- File formats: JPG, PNG, or vector (SVG, AI)

## NOTES FOR ILLUSTRATOR

1. Ensure no text in images is using emdashes or hyphens where possible, or use standard characters
2. Use clear, readable fonts for labels
3. Include scale references where appropriate (size comparisons)
4. Maintain scientific accuracy where necessary
5. Make illustrations engaging and not intimidating
6. Include diverse representation in human figures
7. Consider what would make a Grade 6 student curious and engaged

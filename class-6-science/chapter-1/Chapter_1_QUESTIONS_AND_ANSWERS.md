## SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS

**Q16. Describe the five steps of the scientific method.**
Answer:
1. Observation: Notice something interesting or not understood
2. Question: Wonder about it and think of a question
3. Guess (Hypothesis): Propose a possible answer
4. Testing: Check the guess through experiments or observations
5. Analysis: Study the results to see if they answer the question

**Q17. Give one real life example (not from the book) of how you used the scientific method to solve a problem. Describe each step.**
Answer: (Student answers may vary. Example)
Problem: My toy was not working
Step 1 (Observe): I noticed the toy did not move even after pressing the button
Step 2 (Question): Why is the toy not working?
Step 3 (Guess): The batteries might be dead
Step 4 (Test): I changed the batteries
Step 5 (Analyze): The toy started working, so my guess was correct

**Q18. What does the chapter mean by "to be a wise person, you must be a whys person"?**
Answer: It means that asking questions (asking "why?") is an important part of being wise and learning. Curiosity and questioning help us understand the world better.

**Q19. Why is curiosity described as the most important thing in science?**
Answer: Curiosity drives us to ask questions, observe carefully, explore, and seek to understand things. Without curiosity, we would not be motivated to do science or make discoveries.

**Q20. The chapter mentions that you will not find all answers to your questions in Grade 6. What does this tell us about science?**
Answer: Science is a continuous journey of learning. There is always more to discover and learn. Science is not limited to one grade or period but continues throughout our lives. Knowledge builds over time, and new discoveries always lead to new questions.

It is not just about memorising facts but about asking questions and finding answers through observation and experimentation. Science is important because it helps us solve everyday problems and make discoveries about our world. Whether we are a cook, a bicycle repair person, an electrician, or a student, we use scientific thinking in our daily lives. Science teaches us to be curious, to observe carefully, and to think logically. By understanding science, we can understand everything from why plants grow to how our bodies work to what the stars are made of. **Q34. Why do you think the chapter emphasizes that "scientists work in teams"? How does collaboration help in scientific discovery? (150 200 words)**

Suggested Answer:
The chapter emphasizes teamwork in science because collaboration helps scientists solve problems more effectively and make better discoveries. When scientists work in teams, they can share ideas and perspectives, which leads to more creative solutions. Different team members might have different experiences and knowledge that can help solve a problem.

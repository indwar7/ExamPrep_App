Detailed Content

Complete chapter with organized sections and key vocabulary

What is Science?

Science is a way of thinking, observing and doing things to understand the world we live in and to uncover the secrets of the universe. Think of it as a big adventure where we ask questions, explore the world and try to understand how things work.

The most important thing is to have Curiosity, which is where the title of this book comes from.

Science as a Jigsaw Puzzle

Science is like a giant and unending jigsaw puzzle. Every new discovery we make adds another piece to that puzzle. The best thing about this puzzle? There is no limit to what we can discover.

Every new piece of knowledge leads to more questions and more things to find out. Sometimes, we find that a piece of this puzzle has been put in the wrong place and needs to be moved. New discoveries often change our understanding of the world.

The Five Steps of Scientific Method

1. Observation: We observe something that we find interesting or we do not understand.

2. Question: This makes us wonder and perhaps think of a question about it.

3. Hypothesis/Guess: Then, we guess a possible answer to that question.

4. Testing: We test this guess through experiments or more observations.

5. Analysis: We then try to analyse the results to see if it actually answers our question.

Key Vocabulary

Science: A way of thinking, observing and doing things to understand the world

Curiosity: The desire to know or learn something

Scientific Method: A systematic process of observation, questioning, guessing, testing, and analyzing

Observation: Noticing something carefully

Hypothesis: A guess or possible answer to a question

Summary Notes

Quick reference guide for revision and quick learning

Core Concept

Science is a way of thinking, observing, and doing things to understand the world. It is driven by curiosity and asking "why?"

What is Science?

  • A journey of exploration and discovery
  • Present everywhere: oceans, space, kitchen, playground
  • Driven by curiosity and asking "why?"
  • Never ending: new discoveries lead to new questions

The Scientific Method (5 Steps)

  • Observe: Notice something interesting
  • Wonder: Think of a question about it
  • Guess: Propose a possible answer
  • Test: Check through experiments
  • Analyze: Study results to see if they answer the question

Who Are Scientists?

Anyone who follows the scientific method is a scientist.

  • A cook wondering why dal spills from a cooker
  • A repair person finding where air leaks from a tire
  • An electrician troubleshooting a non working light bulb
  • You, when you ask questions and find answers

Main Message

To be a wise person, you must be a "whys" person!

Questions & Answers

Practice questions for self assessment and learning

Multiple Choice Questions

Q1.
What is the most important thing needed to do science?
Answer: C. Curiosity
Q2.
How many steps are there in the scientific method?
Answer: C. Five
Q3.
Which of the following is an example of a scientist in everyday life?
Answer: D. All of the above (cook, bicycle repair person, electrician)

Short Answer Questions

Q4.
Describe the five steps of the scientific method.
Answer: 1) Observation 2) Question 3) Guess/Hypothesis 4) Testing 5) Analysis
Q5.
Why is curiosity described as the most important thing in science?
Answer: Curiosity drives us to ask questions, observe carefully, explore, and seek to understand things.

True or False

Q6.
Only people with special training can do science.
Answer: False. Anyone who follows the scientific method can do science.
Q7.
New discoveries often change our understanding of the world.
Answer: True
